Geranium, specifically Pelargonium species, is often used for its medicinal properties. Here are some ways to use geranium as a medicinal plant and the conditions it may help:

Essential Oil:
Application: Geranium essential oil is commonly used in aromatherapy and can be applied topically when diluted with a carrier oil.
Benefits: It is believed to have anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties. It is often used to promote emotional well-being and balance hormones.

Tea:
Preparation: Geranium leaves can be used to make a soothing herbal tea.
Benefits: Geranium tea may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues, such as coughs and sore throats. It is also believed to have mild diuretic properties.

Skin Conditions:
Topical Application: Geranium oil can be diluted and applied to the skin for various skin conditions.
Benefits: It may help with conditions like acne, dermatitis, and eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Anti-Anxiety:
Aromatherapy: Inhaling the aroma of geranium essential oil may have calming effects.
Benefits: Geranium is sometimes used in aromatherapy to reduce anxiety and stress.

Wound Healing:
Topical Use: The antimicrobial properties of geranium oil may aid in wound healing.
Benefits: It can be applied to minor cuts and wounds to prevent infection and promote faster healing.
It's important to note that while geranium may offer various health benefits, it is not a cure for specific diseases. Always consult with a healthcare professional 
before using geranium or any other medicinal plant for therapeutic purposes, especially if you have existing health conditions or are taking medications.

Additionally, individual responses to herbal remedies can vary, and what works for one person may not work for another. It's crucial to use caution, perform a patch 
test when using essential oils, and adhere to recommended dilution guidelines to avoid adverse reactions.
